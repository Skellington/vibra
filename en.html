If there's
energy transition, it's

Our goal is to play a leading role in the development of a low-carbon economy in brazil.

We have repositioned ourselves as an energy company that will prioritize the transition to cleaner, renewable energy sources. Thus, social, environmental and governance (ESG) issues are vital to Vibra and are at the center of our priorities, directing the adaptation of our business, processes and new challenges.

Our strategy is to strengthen our current portfolio, through investment in new growth vectors and gradually betting on innovation spaces. We will contribute with our expertise as a distributor, trader, successful logistics manager and our ability to establish sustainable partnerships for the new businesses into which we will enter.

Already in our first year as an energy company, we started to strategically occupy the spaces foreseen in our planning, in segments that demonstrate high growth potential in the coming years.

  • Ethanol: joint venture with copersucar makes us the largest platform for ethanol origination and sales in Brazil and one of the largest in the world
  • Biomethane: cooperation agreement with zeg biogas for the supply of an integrated solution for the biomethane production from vinasse
  • Energy: acquisition of targus energia (one of the 25 largest independent energy traders in the country) and 50% of COMERC (one of the main energy traders in Brazil)
  • Electromobility: funding support of the Easy Volt startup, , a company that has one of the largest electric recharge networks in Brazil
  • Green diesel: partnership with Brasil BioFuels for the creation of Brazil's first green diesel biorefinery, planned for 2025
  • Renewable diesel: pilot project under development in Paraná

Corporate governance

We always seek to improve ourselves to ensure we have with the best governance practices. As a publicly-traded company, in 2021 we perceived the opportunity to rethink how we structure our main governance points. The challenge was to adapt our instruments to the new reality of a modern company, which demands speedy decision-making without losing the rigorous controls that we always practice.

Vibra does not tolerate corruption. We invest in communication and training in anti-corruption policies and procedures

  • 100% of the members of the governance body trained
  • 100% of employees communicated
  • 87% of employees trained

KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS:

  • New Integrity Policy
  • Code of Ethical Conduct Review
  • Creation of the Ethics Committee
  • Implementation of the Privacy and Personal Data Governance Program
  • Structuring the new Integrity Trail - set of training courses related to ethics, compliance, risk management, governance and LGPD

Social commitment

In the second year of the pandemic, we continued to implement actions to support Brazil in the fight against Covid-19, in addition to maintaining our volunteer projects.

KEY ACCOMPLISHMENTS:

  • Donation of medical refrigerators for the storage of immunizers for 29 municipalities in the Northeast, benefiting about 560,000 inhabitants.
  • Support for the Circo Crescer e Viver Project, which serve more than 200 children and young people from communities neighboring our headquarters in Rio de Janeiro
  • Support for two projects carried out by the Instituto Meta Educação: Mulheres Artesãs do Estácio, helping craftswomen, and the Reforço do Futuro initiative, serving 40 women and 120 and children.
  • Donation of basic food hampers to communities neighboring our bases in the cities of Duque de Caxias (RJ), Caracaraí (RR), Crato (CE), Cuiabá (MT) and Cruzeiro do Sul (AC), serving 3,120 families and benefiting 697 family farmers
  • 57 Vibra volunteers mentored public school students in Rio de Janeiro through the Trilha Empreendedora (Entrepreneurial Trail) program.
  • Logistical support for the Central Única das Favelas (CUFA) to transport 24 tons of food, a thousand basic hampers and 100 kg of clothes in the SOS Petrópolis Campaign
  • In partnership with Vicar (Stock Car organizer), and CUFA, a donation campaign was carried out during g the transmission of the Stok Car race in Goiânia, where cars competed in the race displaying the #AJUDEPETROPOLIS sticker.

Environmental
management

We constantly work to assure legal compliance in our operations, reduce the use of natural resources and the waste generation in our processes, minimize atmospheric emissions, preserve biodiversity, prevent and mitigate environmental liabilities, develop projects aimed at energy efficiency and the bolstering of environmental education within the Company.

Energy

TARGETS FOR 2024:

  • Reduce 10% in the energy consumption of our distribution bases
  • Increase energy efficiency within our organization by at least 4%, compared to the base year 2019

MAIN ACHIEVEMENTS IN 2021:

  • 7 Vibra facilities had already migrated to the free energy market and started consuming electricity from renewable energy sources
  • We purchased 11,000 I-RECs (International Renewable Energy Certificates), which is equivalent to 11,000 MWh of electricity produced from renewable sources.

In 2021, we are committed to having zero net emissions for scopes 1 and 2 by the year 2025 and the ambitious net zero emission target for scope 3 by 2050.

Climate change

REDUCTION TARGET BY 2026:

  • Reduce Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 67%, compared to the base year 2019

A large part of this reduction is linked to the deactivation of a thermal plant located in the north of Brazil, which is expected to be serviced by the national interconnected system in the next few years with the transmission line works completion.

MAIN ACHIEVEMENTS IN 2021:

  • expansion of the portfolio to offer carbon intensity products and services
  • creation of the Climate Change Plan within the Vibra's Strategic ESG Agenda, with short- and medium-term reduction goals
  • We were included in the Climate Resilience Index - ICDPR70, due to our performance in the CDP and our placement among the B3's 100 most liquid companies

Although the organization's production processes as a whole do not involve intensive use of water, we continue to act on some fronts to preserve water resources

Water and effluents

TARGET BY 2024:

  • Reduce the total catchment of our facilities by at least 10% compared to the consumption in 2019

MAIN ACHIEVEMENTS IN 2021:

  • geographical mapping of the units that are located in areas of water stress. Result: water abstraction in these locations represents less than 1% of the total volume abstracted for our operations in 2021.
  • 2,000,000 L of rainwater collected and treated for reuse

Our management of materials usage and waste generation is a permanent point of attention and care, based on the National Solid Waste Policy and guided by our Safety, Health and Environment Policy (HSE).

Waste and materials

MAIN ACHIEVEMENTS IN 2021:

  • approximately 412 tons of empty drums sent to recovery operations
  • 29% of the total 1L plastic packaging sold was collected and sent for recycling (percentage above 20% target initially stipulated for the year)
  • Acquisition of 4,510 m3 of refined base oil to be used in our formulations
  • 34% of total waste generated at our administrative and operational facilities were sent for recovery operations (reuse, recycling and others), totaling 1,555 tons
